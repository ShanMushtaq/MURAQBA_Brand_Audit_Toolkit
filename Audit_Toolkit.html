What is a Brand Audit?

A brand audit is a deep examination of a brand's current position in the market compared to where it should be. It looks at everything the brand says, shows, and does — across all touchpoints — to find gaps, inconsistencies, and opportunities. As a digital advertising agency, this is your most powerful tool before recommending any service or package.

Section 02
Mission, Vision & Values
Every strong brand knows WHY it exists, WHERE it's going, and HOW it behaves. Without these, all marketing is guesswork. Audit whether the brand has clarity here.
⚠️

Most Common Issue

90% of small Pakistani brands have no documented mission or vision. They started selling without defining their purpose. This is not a problem — it's your opportunity to help them build it as part of your service.

Mission Statement
Brand has a clear mission statement Critical
A mission answers: Why does this brand exist? What problem does it solve for its customers?
Mission is customer-focused, not just profit-focused
"To help women feel confident everyday" vs "To sell clothes" — which is more powerful?
Mission is visible or reflected in their content
Even if not written, do their posts communicate a clear purpose?
Vision Statement
Brand has a future vision (even informally stated)
Where does the founder want this brand to be in 3–5 years?
Vision is ambitious but believable
"Become Pakistan's top modest wear brand by 2027" — specific and aspirational
Brand Values
Brand has 3–5 core values identified
E.g. Quality, Modesty, Affordability, Speed, Community. These guide all decisions.
Values are reflected in how they talk to customers
A brand that values "kindness" should have warm, welcoming captions and replies
Values are reflected in product quality and pricing
If they say "premium quality," does the product and price point match?
Unique Value Proposition (USP)
Brand can clearly answer: "Why you, not your competitor?" Critical
If they can't answer this in one sentence, neither can their customers. Major problem.
USP is communicated in bio, captions, and ads
Customers should understand the unique advantage without asking
USP is genuinely different, not just "good quality and low price"
That's not a USP — every brand claims that. What makes this one ACTUALLY different?
Section 03
Brand Identity & Visual System
Visual identity is the face of the brand — the first thing anyone sees. Check every element that a customer encounters visually, from logo to product photos.
Logo
Brand has a logo Critical
Even a basic text logo counts. A brand with no logo is invisible in a crowded feed.
Logo is clear and readable at small sizes (profile picture size)
Test: screenshot the profile picture and view it at 40x40px. Is it still recognizable?
Logo has a clean version on white AND dark background
They'll need both for different uses — posts, products, packaging
Logo reflects brand personality (luxury, playful, minimal, bold)
A handwritten logo suggests warmth. A geometric logo suggests structure.
Logo does NOT look copied, generic, or created with a free logo maker
Canva free logos with generic icons look unprofessional. Check for this.
Color Palette
Brand has 2–4 consistent colors used across all posts Important
Scroll their Instagram grid — do you see a consistent color story, or visual chaos?
Color palette matches the brand's target customer and tone
Soft pastels = feminine/gentle. Bold primary colors = energetic/affordable. Earth tones = natural/premium.
Same colors are used in both organic posts AND any ads
Inconsistency between organic and paid content breaks brand trust
Typography (Fonts)
Brand uses consistent fonts in posts and graphics
Changing fonts in every post = no brand personality
Fonts are readable and appropriate for the niche
Decorative scripts are pretty but unreadable at small sizes — balance style with function
Product Photography & Visual Quality
Product photos are high quality and well-lit Critical
Bad product photos kill even the best ads. This is non-negotiable before running ads.
Photos have consistent style (same background, same lighting mood)
A mix of white background, outdoor, and random indoor shots looks unpolished
Human/model photos are included (not just flat lays)
Clothing brands sell better when people can see how items look worn. Always recommend this.
Photos do not have visible watermarks from competitors or photographer issues
Stolen photos are a real problem in Pakistan's clothing market. Check this.

Section 04
Brand Guidelines & Internal Consistency
Brand guidelines are the rulebook that keeps a brand looking and sounding the same everywhere. Without them, every new post, ad, or collaboration pulls the brand in a different direction.
💡

MURAQBA Opportunity

Most small brands don't have brand guidelines. Creating a simple 1-page brand guide (logo, colors, fonts, tone) can be offered as a add-on service and positions you as a premium agency.

Brand Guidelines Document
Brand has any form of documented guidelines
Even a simple PDF with logo, colors, and fonts. Most brands don't — note it.
Logo usage rules are defined (minimum size, clear space, no-stretch rule)
Stretched or tiny logos are a sign of no guidelines
Color codes (HEX or RGB) are saved and accessible
Without the exact color code, every designer recreates a slightly different shade
Font names are documented for future use
Otherwise, the brand font becomes "the one that looks like the old posts"
Cross-Platform Visual Consistency
Facebook Page and Instagram look like the same brand Important
Same profile picture, same bio style, same color story — often completely different
Profile picture is the same (or coordinated) across platforms
Different profile pictures across platforms destroys brand recognition
Cover photo / banner is consistent, branded, and up to date
A blank cover photo or unbranded banner is a major missed opportunity
Ads match the organic content visual style
When ads look completely different to organic posts, it breaks the brand experience
WhatsApp Business profile is branded (logo, description, catalog)
Many Pakistan brands take orders via WhatsApp — check if this touchpoint is branded
Packaging / labels are consistent with digital branding (if physical product)
Unboxing experience = a massive untapped brand touchpoint for clothing brands
Templates & Systems
Brand uses content templates (not designing every post from scratch)
Templates = faster content, more consistent look, less decision fatigue
Brand has saved Canva / design files for their own templates
If only you have the files, the client is dependent on you — great for retention but flag it
Watermark / logo is placed consistently on product photos
Prevents photo theft and increases brand recall when posts are shared
Section 05
Brand Voice & Communication Style
Brand voice is HOW the brand speaks — the personality behind every caption, reply, and story. A brand that sounds like a real person builds trust faster than one that sounds like a catalogue.
Brand Voice Pillars (Examples)

Warm & Friendly — Feels like advice from a friend
Authoritative — Confident, knowledgeable, trusted expert
Playful — Light, fun, uses humour
Inspirational — Empowers and motivates
Elegant — Refined, luxury, aspirational
Direct — No fluff, gets straight to the point

3 Ways to Test Brand Voice

1. Read 10 captions aloud — do they all sound like the same person?
2. Check comment replies — is the brand warm or cold?
3. Look at DM screenshots (if shared) — is the sales approach consistent?

Caption & Content Writing Quality
Captions go beyond "DM to order" or product description only Important
Value-adding captions (tips, stories, questions) drive engagement. "Price PKR 2,500" is not a caption.
Captions have a consistent call-to-action (CTA)
Every caption should tell the reader what to do next: DM, comment, click link, etc.
Writing style is consistent across all posts
Some formal, some casual, some in Urdu, some in English — is there a pattern?
Hashtag strategy is consistent and relevant
30 random hashtags ≠ strategy. Check if hashtags are niche-specific and mixed by size.
Language choice (Urdu / English / mix) is intentional
Urdu reaches broader local audiences. English signals premium. Know which this brand should use.
Customer Interaction & DM Management
Brand replies to comments consistently Important
Comments left unanswered = lost engagement + bad signal to algorithm
DM response time is under 3 hours during business hours
In Pakistan's DM-based commerce, slow replies = lost sales. Ask the client directly.
Reply tone in comments is warm and professional
Even negative comments deserve a professional, calm response
Brand does NOT get into arguments or delete critical comments
Deleting real negative comments destroys trust faster than the original complaint
Story polls, questions, and interactive stickers are used
Interactive stories = higher engagement rate + better algorithm reach
Section 06
Digital Presence Audit
Go through each platform systematically. You're checking for completeness, professionalism, and whether each platform is actually working for the brand or just existing.
Instagram Profile
Username is the brand name (not personal name or random numbers) Critical
@zarascollection is searchable. @user38192847 is not a brand.
Bio clearly states: what they sell, who it's for, and how to buy
A good bio answers all 3 in 2 lines. Check if the client's bio does this.
Link in bio is active and leads somewhere useful
WhatsApp link, Linktree, or website — not empty or broken
Account is set to Business or Creator (not Personal)
Business account = access to insights, ads, contact buttons. Non-negotiable for a brand.
Highlights are organized and labeled clearly
Highlights for: Products, Reviews, Process, FAQ, About — not a pile of random expired stories
Feed has at least 9 posts (a full first impression row)
When someone visits, they see a 3x3 grid. Is that grid appealing?
Posting frequency is at least 3x/week
Brands that post once a month are invisible. Algorithm punishes infrequent accounts.
Reels are being used (not just static posts)
Reels get 40–60% more reach than static posts on Instagram in 2025
Facebook Page
Page is a Business Page (not a personal profile used for business) Critical
You CANNOT run ads from a personal profile. This is a hard requirement.
Page category is correct (Clothing, Online Store, etc.)
Wrong category affects discoverability and how the page shows up in search
About section is fully filled (website, contact, description)
Incomplete About sections look like abandoned pages
Page is connected to Instagram (for running ads across both)
Unconnected pages can't run Instagram ads from Meta Ads Manager
Page has Meta Pixel installed (if they have a website)
No pixel = no retargeting = leaving money on the table
Overall Engagement Health
Engagement rate is above 2% (likes + comments ÷ followers)
Under 1% means followers are not engaged — possibly bought followers or dead account
Follower growth is steady (not a sudden spike then flat)
A sudden spike followed by flatline = bought followers. Show this to clients if they claim "organic growth"
Stories are posted at least 3–4 times per week
Stories keep you at the top of your followers' feeds. No stories = no daily visibility.
Section 07
Content & Marketing Effectiveness
Great content is the engine of every brand. Check whether the content this brand is creating is actually working — educating, engaging, converting, and building loyalty.
Content Mix & Strategy
Content is a mix — not 100% product selling posts Important
Best mix: 40% product/sales, 30% educational/tips, 20% social proof/reviews, 10% behind-the-scenes
Brand posts behind-the-scenes content (packing, process, founder)
Humanizing the brand increases trust — especially in Pakistan's DM-based market
Educational or value-adding content is included
E.g. "How to style an abaya 3 ways" — this gets saved and shared, not just liked
Customer testimonials / reviews are posted regularly
Social proof is the most powerful sales tool. If they have happy customers, show them.
Seasonal and trend-relevant content is planned ahead
Eid, winter, back to school — brands that plan ahead dominate brands that react late
Content Calendar & Consistency
Brand has any form of content calendar or planned schedule
Even a simple WhatsApp reminder to post is better than nothing
Posting schedule is consistent (same days/times each week)
Algorithm rewards consistency. Random posting = algorithm punishes you.
Best performing post types have been identified and repeated
What gets the most saves, shares, comments? Do more of that. Has the brand noticed?
Marketing Communication
All promotional posts have a clear offer and deadline
"50% off today only" beats "we have a sale going on." Urgency matters.
WhatsApp broadcast / Story is used for promotions
Existing customers are the easiest sale. Are they being communicated with directly?
At least one lead magnet or freebie has been tested
E.g. "DM us 'LOOKBOOK' for our free styling guide" — builds DM list and conversation

Section 08
External Perception & Customer Feedback
What do customers actually think about this brand? External perception is what people say when the brand isn't in the room. It's often very different from what the brand believes about itself.
⚠️

Reality Check

Many brands believe they have a great reputation because they see their own happy customer screenshots. A real audit means looking for the negative too — bad comments, deleted reviews, angry DM screenshots, or any public complaints. This honesty is what makes your audit valuable.

Reviews & Testimonials
Facebook page has reviews/recommendations visible Important
Open the page — can you see a star rating? What is it? Are there written reviews?
Customer testimonials are actively collected and posted
Brands that share real screenshots of happy customers build more trust than any ad
Negative reviews/feedback have been responded to professionally
A brand that handles complaints well publicly actually builds MORE trust than one with none
There are no patterns of complaints (late delivery, wrong item, etc.)
If 5 people complain about the same thing, it's not bad luck — it's a process problem
Social Sentiment Analysis
Comment section tone is predominantly positive
Read the last 50 comments across recent posts — what's the mood?
Customers tag this brand in their own posts (organic UGC)
User-Generated Content = free marketing + proof of real customers. Is it happening?
Brand is mentioned or discussed in relevant Facebook groups
Search the brand name in Pakistani clothing buyer groups — what comes up?
No recent viral complaints or boycott posts found
Search brand name + "scam", "fraud", "beware" — unfortunately common in Pakistan e-commerce
Customer Experience Touchpoints
Ordering process is clear and easy (DM / website / catalog)
Ask the client: "Walk me through exactly how a customer orders." Complexity = lost sales.
Delivery is within the promised timeframe
Late delivery is the #1 source of negative reviews in Pakistan's online market
Post-purchase follow up exists (thank you message, review request)
Most brands disappear after payment. A single "how's your order?" message = massive goodwill.
Return / exchange policy is clearly communicated
Unclear return policies cause anxiety before purchase — kills conversions
Section 09
Brand Recognition & Loyalty
Recognition is whether people remember the brand. Loyalty is whether people come back and refer others. Both take time to build — but both can be measured and accelerated.
Brand Recognition Signals
Brand has a recognizable visual signature (color, style, aesthetic)
Can you identify this brand's post without seeing the logo? That's brand recognition.
Brand uses a consistent, searchable hashtag of their own
E.g. #ZarasCollection — a custom hashtag that aggregates all their content
Customers mention the brand by name in comments
"I love Zara's abayas!" in comments = high awareness. Vague "nice post" = low awareness.
Brand shows up when you search their name on Facebook/Instagram
Simple test — search the brand name. Are they easy to find?
Brand Loyalty Signals
Repeat customers exist (not just one-time buyers) Important
Ask the client: "Do you have regular customers who come back?" If no — why not?
Referrals are happening (customers bringing new customers)
"My friend told me about you" = the most powerful and free marketing channel
Brand has a loyalty incentive (discount for repeat buyers, referral reward)
A simple "bring a friend, both get 10% off" can double word-of-mouth overnight
Customers actively engage with stories and posts (saves, shares)
Saves = strong interest. Shares = brand advocacy. These are the highest loyalty signals.
Brand has any form of community (WhatsApp group, Facebook group)
A loyal community of even 100 people is worth more than 10,000 passive followers
Brand celebrates customers (reshares their photos, mentions them)
Brands that make customers feel seen create fans, not just buyers

Section 10
Competitive Positioning
No brand exists in isolation. Where does this brand sit relative to its competitors? Is it differentiated, or is it just another version of the same thing? This section gives you the data to answer that.
💡

How to Find Competitors

Search Instagram hashtags (#lahoriclothing, #pakistaniabaya, etc.) → look at similar accounts. Check who follows and engages with the client's page. Ask the client: "Who do you look at for inspiration? Who are you competing with?"

CompetitorFollowersPrice RangeTheir StrengthTheir WeaknessYour Client's Edge
Competitive Differentiation
Brand looks visually different from its top 3 competitors Critical
Open all competitor profiles side by side. Does this brand stand out or blend in?
Brand's price point is justified by its perceived quality
Pricing above average? The brand must look premium. Pricing below? Volume game — is that intentional?
Brand targets a niche the competitors are ignoring
E.g. competitors target all women — this brand targets plus-size modest wear. Niche = less competition.
Brand has a content angle competitors don't use
Unique content formats, behind-the-scenes, founder story — something competitors are missing
Market Positioning
Brand clearly occupies one of these positions: Budget / Mid-range / Premium
Brands stuck between positions confuse buyers. Pick a lane and own it.
Brand has identified its primary geographic market (Lahore, all Pakistan, diaspora)
Targeting "everyone" means reaching no one. A specific geographic focus improves ad targeting precision.
Brand tracks what competitors are doing (new launches, campaigns)
Not to copy — to spot gaps. When all competitors ignore Eid prep, being first wins.
Section 11
Ad Account & Paid Performance Audit
If the client is running ads — or has run ads before — this section is critical. Poor ad performance is often not the ad's fault. It's the targeting, the landing page, the offer, or the creative quality.
📌

If They've Never Run Ads

Skip the performance metrics — but still fill in the setup checklist. A brand that's never run ads often has account issues that would prevent launching ads immediately. Find these now, not after they've paid you.

Ad Account Setup
Meta Business Manager account exists and is set up Critical
Cannot run ads without Business Manager. Many small brands have never set it up.
Ad Account is active and in good standing (no restrictions) Critical
Restricted accounts cannot run ads. Check this FIRST before promising anything.
Valid payment method added to Ad Account
Debit card, credit card, or Meta credit — must be active and not expired
Facebook Page is connected to Business Manager
Pages not in Business Manager create ad management complications
Instagram Account is connected to Business Manager
Required to run Instagram ads properly
Meta Pixel is installed (if they have a website)
Without pixel: no retargeting, no purchase tracking, no lookalike audiences from buyers
Previous Ad Performance (if applicable)
CPM (Cost Per 1,000 Impressions) is within acceptable range
Pakistan average: PKR 150–400 CPM for clothing. Above PKR 800 = targeting or creative issue.
CTR (Click-Through Rate) is above 1%
Below 0.5% CTR means the ad creative or offer is not compelling enough
Ads were using the correct campaign objective
Running a "Page Likes" objective to get sales is one of the most common beginner mistakes
Audience targeting was specific (not "Pakistan, 18–65, All Interests")
Broad targeting wastes budget. Interest + behavior + lookalike layering is the correct approach.
Ad creative was tested (A/B test with different images or copy)
Running one ad with no testing = leaving optimization on the table
Results were tracked (cost per lead / cost per DM / cost per sale)
"The ad got 500 likes" is not a business result. Revenue and leads are what matter.
Section 12
Internal Team & Brand Awareness
For small brands in Pakistan, "internal" often means the founder and maybe 1–2 family members. Still — does everyone involved in the business understand and represent the brand consistently?
Founder Brand Clarity
Founder can describe the brand in one sentence without hesitating Important
If the founder can't, their team and customers definitely can't
Founder knows who their ideal customer is (age, location, income, interests)
Vague answer ("women who like fashion") = vague targeting in ads = wasted budget
Founder is aware of what makes them different from competitors
If they can't name 2 genuine differences, you have work to do before ads can work
Founder tracks basic numbers (monthly sales, DMs, conversion rate)
A founder who doesn't track numbers cannot tell you if ads are working
Team & Operations (if any team exists)
Anyone helping with the brand knows the brand voice
A family member replying to DMs in a completely different tone = inconsistent brand experience
Content creation is not solely dependent on one person
If the founder disappears for a week, does posting stop? Single points of failure = risky.
There is a process for handling orders, complaints, and returns
Inconsistent handling of orders is a sign of no system — this causes negative reviews
Brand Growth Mindset
Founder is open to feedback and change
A client who refuses all suggestions is not a good client for MURAQBA's results model
Founder has realistic expectations about ad timelines (14–30 days for results)
Clients expecting results in 3 days will always be disappointed — set expectations now
Founder is willing to provide quality assets (photos, videos) on time
Your biggest bottleneck as an agency is often waiting for client assets. Assess this risk early.
